ADAM's BIKES

Summit Metroparks Freedom Trail

This trail is own by Summit Metro Parks. It follow an old railbed start near Portage Hike & Bike Trail and extends to Lincoln Street in Akron, Ohio. This old railbed provides the bike rider with scenic green spaces. Bald eagles, coyote and white-tail deer use these green spaces as their home.

Ohio and Erie Towpath

The Ohio & Erie Canal towpath follows the historic Ohio & Erie Canal for 87 miles. It goes through four countys in Northeast Ohio starting at the Great Lake of Erie and ending at highway 800 in Tuscarawas County. There is much to see in the 87 mile towpath as it winds through Cleveland Metroparks, Cuyahoga Valley National Park, Summit Metroparks, Stark county park district and Tuscaraws County. This is a bike trail like no other because you are actually seeing the early history of Ohio. There are remenants of the locks that were used to push the boats along their path. There are historic town such as Canal Fulton and Zoar to visit. Part of the towpath even floats out on Lake Portage. Through the Summit Metropark and Cuyahoga Valley National Park the bike rider has the option of riding the towpath one way and taking the train back for a small fee. It is a bike rider to remember.

Bike and Hike Trail

The Bike and Hike trail follows the the course of the old Akron, Bedford & Cleveland Railroad from Hudson Drive to Stow north of Route 8 in Boston Heights. The path runs along The Cuyahoga River. Along the river you can see blue herons and Canada Geese. South of Boston Mills Road in Boston Heights, The Sharon Conglomerate rock walls of the Boston Ledges rise along the trail.
